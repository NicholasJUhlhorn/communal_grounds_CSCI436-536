Research Methodology

Systematic Literature Review

We conducted a systematic literature review to better understand the problem space of community engagement and participation. We reviewed the following papers:

  • The City Residents Do Not Get Involved: Understanding Barriers to Community Participation in a Small Texas Boomtown
  • Coalesce: An Accessible Mixed-Initiative System for Designing Community-Centric Questionnaires
  • Following the evolution of distributed Communities of Practice
  • User Role Discovery Method in Virtual Community Based on DBSCAN Clustering
  • Humanitarian Application of Science and Technology
  • Human Smart Cities: A Human-centric model aiming at the wellbeing and quality of life of citizens

These papers helped us each individually create our initial designs for Communal Grounds. We then combined our ideas together into a first prototype mockup of the site. See the prototype images.

System Analysis

We analyzed nine existing systems and platforms to identify their strengths and weaknesses in relation to our goals. The systems analyzed included: Reddit, Discord, Volunteero, Mobilize, Craigslist, Idealist - VolunteerMatch, Discourse, Circle, and Slack.

Our analysis revealed that current solutions either lacked a community-building aspect, were too restrictive or static, or failed to provide a complete, non-political platform for in-app planning and communication. This confirmed the need for Communal Grounds as a dedicated, free, community-managed, and comprehensive solution.

Using our findings we refined our prototype each individually again. Once we had our extended prototypes we once again combined them all into a single prototype.

User Study

We conducted in-depth user interviews to validate our initial design ideas and identify key user needs. We also had our participants engage in co-designs with us to illuminate solutions or ideas we may have overlooked while building out our prototypes.

Findings from the interviews include:

  • The ability for organizers to query a "volunteer pool" for short-notice work based on availability and location.
  • Expanded communication channels, such as a wiki-style format for project pages with specialized channels for updates.
  • A system for work delegation and explicit role assignment (job titles) to ensure clear responsibilities within a project.
  • It's important to make our solution unique and valuable enough to where users won't feel like they're adding bloat to their routine
  • Make dates easily viewable in order to help with organization and routine.
  • Add polls in order to give users more input (this was something the interviewee's existing tech option already had and I personally liked it and felt like it fit our solution)

We then followed our usual steps to create refined prototypes then combine them all into our final prototype; from which we started building out Communal Grounds.
